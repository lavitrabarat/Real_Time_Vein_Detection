# Real_Time_Vein_Detection
Developed by Team Biolight Pioneers, the **Hand Vein-Detection System** is an innovative, low-cost, and portable medical device engineered to address the 10–50% failure rate associated with first-attempt venipuncture in patients with Difficult Venous Access (DVA), such as those experiencing obesity, dark skin tones, or dehydration. The system integrates a Raspberry Pi 4 Model B+ microcontroller with an Infrared (IR) Camera and high-intensity IR LEDs to capture subsurface blood vessel structures, leveraging the principle that deoxygenated hemoglobin absorbs near-infrared light differently than surrounding tissue. A custom-built OpenCV software pipeline enhances the raw image feed using Contrast Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization (CLAHE), segments it via adaptive Otsu thresholding, and eliminates noise with median filtering to map the target vein with high contrast. Once localized, a precise motion profile coordinates dual servo motors on a rack and pinion mechanism to physically project a safe laser diode indicator directly onto the patient's skin, incorporating live safety loops and an emergency stop to ensure maximum accuracy and patient safety. Ultimately, this power-efficient and highly affordable design democratizes healthcare by reducing human error and painful needle-insertion attempts in small clinics, remote field hospitals, and low-resource environments.
